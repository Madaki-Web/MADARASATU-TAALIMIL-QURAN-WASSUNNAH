Prophets in the Quran

The Quran mentions 25 prophets by name, each with their own story and significance.

Major Prophets and Their Stories

  • Adam: The first human being and prophet, mentioned 25 times.
  • Noah (Nuh): Preached for 950 years, mentioned 43 times.
  • Abraham (Ibrahim): "Khalil Allah" (the friend of Allah), mentioned 69 times.
  • Moses (Musa): Received the Torah, mentioned 136 times.
  • Jesus (Isa): Born without a father, mentioned 25 times.

Other Prophets with Books

  • Moses (Musa): Torah (Tawrat)
  • David (Dawud): Psalms (Zabur)
  • Jesus (Isa): Gospel (Injil)
  • Abraham (Ibrahim): Scrolls (Suhuf)

Islamic belief holds that Allah sent 124,000 prophets. Muhammad (S.A.W) is the final messenger.

Angels in Islam

Creation of Angels

Angels are spiritual beings created from light. They obey Allah and never disobey His command.

Duties of Angels

  • Worshiping and glorifying Allah constantly.
  • Delivering revelations to prophets (e.g., Jibril).
  • Recording human deeds.
  • Protecting and guiding humans.

Key Angels

  1. Jibril (Gabriel): Delivered revelations.
  2. Mikail (Michael): Oversees sustenance and weather.
  3. Israfil: Will blow the trumpet on the Day of Judgment.
  4. Azrael: Angel of Death.

Surah Al-Fatir (35:1) mentions angels with two, three, or four wings.

Sahabas (Companions of Prophet Muhammad S.A.W)

Who is a Sahaba?

To be considered a Sahaba, a person must:

  1. Have lived during the time of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.W).
  2. Believed in his message (Islam).
  3. Died as a Muslim.

Prophets in the Quran

The Quran mentions 25 prophets by name, each with their own story and significance. Here are some key points about the prophets in the Quran:

Major Prophets and Their Stories

  • Adam: The first human being and prophet, mentioned 25 times in the Quran.
  • Noah (Nuh): A prophet who preached for 950 years, mentioned 43 times.
  • Abraham (Ibrahim): Known as "Khalil Allah" (the friend of Allah), mentioned 69 times.
  • Moses (Musa): Received the Torah and was sent to Pharaoh, mentioned 136 times.
  • Jesus (Isa): Born without a father, mentioned 25 times.

Other Prophets

  • Prophets with nicknames:
    • Abraham (Ibrahim): "Khalil Allah" (the friend of Allah).
  • Prophets with revealed books:
    • Moses (Musa): Received the Torah (Tawrat).
    • David (Dawud): Received the Psalms (Zabur).
    • Jesus (Isa): Received the Gospel (Injil).
    • Abraham (Ibrahim): Received the Scrolls (Suhuf).

Number of Prophets

According to Islamic belief, God sent a total of 124,000 prophets to different nations and tribes throughout history. Prophet Muhammad was the last and final prophet, sent to guide all of humanity.

Chosen Prophets Closest to Allah

While the Quran doesn't specify a number, it does mention that some prophets were preferred over others. Prophet Muhammad is considered the "Seal of the Prophets" and the final messenger of Allah.

Arab Prophets

Four Arab prophets mentioned in the Quran:

  • Hud: Sent to the ʿĀd tribe.
  • Salih: Sent to the Thamud tribe.
  • Shu'ayb: Sent to the Midian people.
  • Muhammad: Sent to all of humanity.

Angels in Islam

Creation of Angels

Angels are considered spiritual beings created by Allah from light. They are mentioned in the Quran and Hadith as beings who serve Allah and carry out His commands.

Duties of Angels

  • Worshiping Allah: Angels are constantly worshiping and glorifying Allah.
  • Carrying out Allah's commands: Including delivering revelations and recording deeds.
  • Protecting humans: Some angels are assigned to protect humans from harm.
  • Recording deeds: Angels record the good and bad deeds of every person.

Angels in the Quran

Surah Al-Fatir (35:1) mentions that angels have wings—two, three, or four.

Angels in Hadith

  • Angel of Death (Azrael): Takes the souls of humans at the time of death.
  • Guardian Angels: Assigned to protect each person.

Key Angels in Islam

  1. Jibril (Gabriel): Delivers revelations to prophets.
  2. Mikail (Michael): Oversees sustenance and natural events.
  3. Israfil: Will blow the trumpet to signal the Day of Judgment.
  4. Azrael: The Angel of Death, takes souls from bodies.

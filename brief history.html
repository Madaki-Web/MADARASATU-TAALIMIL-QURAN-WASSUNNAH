History

Madarasatul-Ta’alimil-Qur’an-was-Sunnah was founded through the efforts and dedication of Malam Abdulkadir Muhammad Ndatsu,
whose Ta’aleemat (Islamic lectures) at Masjid Ja’afar Mahmud Adam in Kwasau, Dutsen Kurangwari,
inspired its establishment. By Allah’s will, these lectures attracted many people,
including some who had previously held misguided beliefs but were rightly guided through his teaching.
This impact moved the community to propose the creation of an Islamiyya school within the mosque, to promote Sunnah and provide children with sound Islamic upbringing—especially since Sunnah-based Islamiyya schools were few in the area.
Responding to this call, Malam Abdulkadir established the school on 5th February 2011 with on ly seven children and five adults as its pioneer students.

Growth and Development

  • 2011: Established with children’s classes (4:00 pm – 6:00 pm) and adults’ classes (Maghrib – Isha’i).
  • 2014: Introduction of the Married Women’s Section, with classes held from 2:00 pm – 4:00 pm.

Subsequent years: Expansion from mosque-based lessons to dedicated classrooms due to increased enrollment.
Today, the Islamiyya has grown to accommodate over 700 students, spread across its three sections:

  1. Children’s Section
  2. Adults’ Section
  3. Married Women’s Section

Mission

To nurture a generation firmly grounded in the Qur’an and Sunnah, guided by authentic Islamic knowledge,
and capable of practicing and spreading the teachings of Islam in their communities.

Vision

To become a leading center of Qur’anic and Islamic education,
where children and adults alike are raised with sound knowledge, upright character, and devotion to Allah, thereby reviving the Sunnah and strengthening the Muslim Ummah.

Curriculum

Our program of study covers a wide range of Islamic sciences, including:
  • ‘Aqeedah (Islamic Creed)
  • Qur’an and Tajweed
  • Adhkar (Supplications & Remembrances)
  • Hadith Studies
  • Fiqh (Islamic Jurisprudence)
  • Seerah (Prophetic Biography)
  • Other Islamic Sciences
  • Special programs include:
  • Qur’an Memorization (Hifz)
  • Daura (Intensive Study Programs)
  • Ta’aleemat from authentic texts, including:
  • Sharh-us-Sunnah
  • Al-Barj
  • Fiqh-us-Sunnah
  • Tariq as-Salihin ila Rabbil-‘Alamin
